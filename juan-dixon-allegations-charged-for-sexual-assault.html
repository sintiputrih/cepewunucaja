Juan Dixon Wikipedia bio

Juan Dixon, a former professional basketball player and coach was born on October 9, 1978, in Baltimore, Maryland.

He was raised by his grandparents, Roberta and Warnick Graves, after his parents, Juanita and Phil, died of AIDS-related illnesses when he was only 17.

Growing up in Baltimore, Juan attended Calvert Hall College High School in Towson, Maryland, where he began playing basketball.

After completing his education, Juan was selected by the Washington Wizards in the second round of the 2002 NBA Draft.

He played for several NBA teams, including the Wizards, the Portland Trail Blazers, the Toronto Raptors, and the Detroit Pistons, before retiring from professional basketball in 2011.

Juan married his high school sweetheart, Robyn Bragg, in July 2005. The couple has two sons, Carter and Corey.

However, they got divorced in March 2012. But in January 2023, the couple remarried and started living together again.

After retiring from basketball, Juan began his coaching career. In November 2013, he joined the Maryland Terrapin coaching staff as a special assistant.

Then, The University of the District of Columbia’s women’s basketball team hired him as head coach in October 2016.

Later, he was hired as the head coach for the men’s basketball team at Coppin State.
