Who was Ryan O’Neal?

Ryan O’Neal was an American actor. Born in Los Angeles, he trained as an amateur boxer before beginning a career in acting in 1960. In 1964, he landed the role of Rodney Harrington on the ABC nighttime soap opera Peyton Place.

Ryan O’Neal biography and career

O’Neal was born in Los Angeles on April 20, 1941, to actress Patricia Ruth Olga and novelist and screenwriter Charles O’Neal. His father had Irish and English heritage, while his mother had Irish and Ashkenazi Jewish ancestry. His brother, Kevin, also pursued a career in acting and screenwriting.

O’Neal attended University High School in Los Angeles, where he trained to become a Golden Gloves boxer. In the late 1950s, his father’s job as a writer on the television series Citizen Soldier led the family to Munich, where O’Neal attended Munich American High School.

In 1964, O’Neal secured the role of Rodney Harrington on the popular ABC nighttime soap opera Peyton Place. This role catapulted his career to new heights. He later achieved success in the film industry, particularly with his performances in Love Story (1970), for which he received nominations for the Academy Award for Best Actor and the Golden Globe Award for Best Actor – Motion Picture Drama. He also made notable appearances in films such as What’s Up, Doc? (1972), Paper Moon (1973), Barry Lyndon (1975), A Bridge Too Far (1977), and The Driver (1978).

From 2005 to 2017, O’Neal had a recurring role as Max, the father of the main character, in the Fox television series Bones.

In 1964, he was chosen to portray Rodney Harrington in the popular television series Peyton Place. O’Neal secured the role because the studio was seeking a young actor similar to Doug McClure. The show achieved great success, propelling its cast, including O’Neal, to national recognition. Many of the cast members, such as Mia Farrow and Barbara Parkins, were offered movie roles. O’Neal himself was eager to venture into the world of films. While working on Peyton Place, O’Neal also appeared in a pilot episode for a potential series called European Eye in 1968. Additionally, he signed a recording contract with ABC. O’Neal’s first leading role in a feature film came in 1969 with The Big Bounce, which was based on an Elmore Leonard novel. That same year, he also appeared in a television adaptation of Under the Yum Yum Tree (1963).

Originally, O’Neal was intended to star in Bogdanovich’s unsuccessful musical At Long Last Love but was ultimately replaced by Burt Reynolds. However, he did collaborate with Reynolds, Bogdanovich, and his daughter Tatum O’Neal in the screwball comedy Nickelodeon (1976), for which he received a fee of $750,000. Unfortunately, the film did not fare well at the box office. O’Neal then took on a minor role in the star-studded war film A Bridge Too Far (1977), portraying General James Gavin. Despite O’Neal’s portrayal being criticized, it is worth noting that he was only one year older than Gavin during the events depicted in the film.
