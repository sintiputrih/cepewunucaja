Revenue From Record Sales Laid The Foundation for Michael Bivins Net Worth of $40 Million

With the many years he has been active in the music industry, it comes as no surprise that Michael Bivins has an extensive body of work. He began his career as one of the founding members of New Edition. The American R&B/pop group has released seven studio albums, two holiday albums, and eight compilations.

Apart from their first album, Candy Girl, the rest received certifications from the Recording Industry Association of America. Their 1984 album New Edition, Heart Break (1988), and Home Again (1996) were all certified platinum twice. In all, it is estimated that New Edition has sold over 20 million records across the globe.

When New Edition went on hiatus in 1989, Bivins teamed up with Ricky Bell and Ronnie DeVoe to form Bell Biv DeVoe (BBD). This music group has released four studio albums thus far and has equally sold millions of records.

Their debut studio album titled Poison was released in March 1990 and went on to pull platinum certification four times. In all, New Edition and Bell Biv DeVoe have made millions of dollars from record sales. It was through this that Bivin first became a millionaire.

The Music Manager Made Good Money From Biv 10 Records Which He Founded

It was sometime in 1992 that Michael Bivins founded his record label, Biv 10 Records. From what we gathered, it was created as a joint venture with Motown Records. It started as Biv Entertainment and recorded multiple successes working with Motown.

Because of this, Motown helped Bivins to create his own imprint rebranded as Biv 10 Records. This afforded him more creative freedom to work with artists and he would go on to record some success with acts like 702 and Subway.

While he never pulled any significant success with the label before it was dismantled in 2012 and folded into Motown, one can’t deny he made good money from the venture. The financial records of the label have never been disclosed to the public but there are no doubts that Biv 10 Records was profitable, at least for a while, and contributed a meaningful amount to Michael Bivin’s $40 million net worth.
