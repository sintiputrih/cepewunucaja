Melissa King - Winner Of 'Top Chef All Stars: L.A'

Publish date: 2024-09-21

The 18th of June 2020 gave a new Top Chef winner. Melissa King walked home with the prestigious title of the Top Chef leaving her competitors Bryan Voltaggio and Stephanie Cmar behind in the finale.

The finale was in Tuscany, Italy, where all three finalists were given tasks alongside the international culinary legends. The judges, Tom Colicchio, Gail Simmons, and Padma Laxmi, asked the finalists to prepare the best progressive four-course meal of their life.

Eventually, Melissa's dish won the hearts of judges. Melissa's work featured the Italian techniques and the local ingredients with the Chinese flavours of her San Gabriel Valley. The elements included char siu glazed octopus with fennel, grilled squab with persimmon, squash agnolotti with Szechuan chilli oil, Hong Kong milk tea tiramisu, and porcini and fermented black bean.

The most exciting thing about the finale is that her final recipes brought the eighth-generation Italian butcher Dario Cecchini into tears. He mentioned that Melissa's works respected Italian traditions. 

Congratulations to Melissa King.

Who Is Melissa King?

Melissa King is a Chinese-American pro chef, who recently became the winner of the Bravo's cooking series season 17 of Top Chef. Previously, she reached to the height of fame after she competed on Bravo's Top Chef in its twelfth season. She made her way to the semi-finals at the time. She is widely acknowledged for her Californian cuisine.

(©: YouTube/Chef Mellisa King)

Melissa, who was also selected as one of the Best Female Chefs in San Francisco, is a brand ambassador for Whole Foods Market.

Melissa was born in Bethany Beach, Delaware, in the United States in 1994, and her birthday is on 29 March. She grew up with her two sisters in California. She is the youngest child of her parents. Her mother belongs to a Cantonese mother while her father hails from a Shanghainese father.

She grew up in the San Gabriel Valley and came up through the Bay Area ranks in kitchens, including Campton Place, Luce, and the Dining Room at the Ritz-Carlton with Ron Siegel. As she grew up, she got her most significant influence on cooking philosophy and career. Melissa was also top at her class at the Culinary Institute of America.

Apart from the cooking world, Melissa King is a former Miss Delaware Teen USA. She graduated from Arizona State University after being transformed from the College of Philadelphia.

Melissa King is a proud gay and a great supporter of LGBTQ rights and dignity. But, whether she has a partner or else lies beyond the mystery.
